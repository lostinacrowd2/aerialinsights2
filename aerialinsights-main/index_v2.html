Precision Positioning

RTK-Corrected Flights as Standard

Every flight we operate uses Real-Time Kinematic (RTK) corrections — an advanced GNSS positioning technology that corrects satellite signal errors in real time during flight. Standard GPS can drift by several feet; RTK narrows that to 1–3 centimeters by comparing your drone's position against a ground-based reference station.

For surveyors and construction teams, this means every Digital Elevation Model, orthomosaic, and 3D point cloud we deliver is accurately georeferenced from the moment it's captured. We also run PPK (Post-Processed Kinematic) as a redundancy layer — if the live correction signal drops mid-flight, your data integrity is still preserved.
